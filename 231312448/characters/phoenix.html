Background

Grew up under Antagonist Party and was trained as a combat medic in the Antagonist military. During off world deployment, she was critically injured and left behind to die by her squad. Having no remaining options to avoid certain death, she tested an experimental angiogenetic drug she'd been researching on herself. She did walk off that battlefield alive, although analysis of her biomonitor blackbox data revealed that she had been clinically dead for just short of a minute. Being abandoned by those she had considered comrades was the breaking point for her, and she fled in a hijacked spaceship, leaving the Antagonist Party behind. The ship's hyperdrive malfunctioned, leaving her stranded adrift in deep space. Just days after her departure, she started noticing symptoms matching those of a malignous tumour. Despite having no perspective of rescue coming, she refused to die, opting instead to perform surgery on herself, extracting the tumour from her chest. This bought her enough time for a Northstar probe to pick up her distress signal during a routine deep space scan, resulting in her being rescued and joining Northstar.

Psychological profile

Suffers from traumatic experiences from her time under the Antagonist military. Strongly despises personally engaging in combat. Impressive perseverance.

Specialisation and skills

Primarily works as combat medic, treating injuries sustained in battle, predominantly ballistic trauma. Also conducts cutting-edge research into new medical technologies. Invented the Phoenix serum that allows treatment of severe trauma that would usually mean certain death, by rapidly overclocking cell regeneration, with the drawback of being carcinogenic. Usually, it would be frowned upon to name an invention after yourself, but an exception can be made if you have literally brought yourself back from the dead.
